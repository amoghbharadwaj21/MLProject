Student Performance Prediction

1) Problem Statement

The goal of this project is to analyze and understand how various factors influence students' test performance. Specifically, the focus is on the following aspects:

  • Performance Analysis: Evaluating how test scores vary with respect to different influencing variables.
  • Influencing Variables:
    • Gender: The gender of the students and its potential impact on test scores.
    • Ethnicity: The ethnic background of students and its correlation with their performance.
    • Parental Level of Education: How the highest level of education attained by the student's parents affects their test scores.
    • Lunch: The type of lunch students receive (standard vs. free/reduced) and its effect on performance.
    • Test Preparation Course: Whether students completed a test preparation course and how this impacts their test scores.

By analyzing these variables, the project aims to uncover patterns and relationships that could provide insights into educational outcomes and help in formulating strategies to improve student performance.

2) Data Collection

Dataset Source: The data is obtained from Kaggle and can be accessed at this link.

Dataset Overview:

  • Columns:
    • Gender: The sex of the student.
      • Male: A student who identifies as male.
      • Female: A student who identifies as female.
    • Race/Ethnicity: The ethnic group to which the student belongs.
      • Group A: Represents a specific ethnic group (Group A).
      • Group B: Represents a specific ethnic group (Group B).
      • Group C: Represents a specific ethnic group (Group C).
      • Group D: Represents a specific ethnic group (Group D).
      • Group E: Represents a specific ethnic group (Group E).
    • Parental Level of Education: The highest level of education achieved by the student's parents.
      • Bachelor's Degree: The parent has obtained a bachelor's degree.
      • Some College: The parent has attended some college but did not complete a degree.
      • Master's Degree: The parent has obtained a master's degree.
      • Associate's Degree: The parent has obtained an associate's degree.
      • High School: The parent has completed high school education.
    • Lunch: The type of lunch the student receives before taking the test.
      • Standard: The student receives a standard lunch.
      • Free/Reduced: The student receives a free or reduced-price lunch.
    • Test Preparation Course: Indicates whether the student completed a test preparation course before taking the test.
      • Completed: The student completed the test preparation course.
      • None: The student did not complete the test preparation course.
    • Math Score: The score achieved by the student in the math exam. This score is a numeric value.
    • Reading Score: The score achieved by the student in the reading exam. This score is a numeric value.
    • Writing Score: The score achieved by the student in the writing exam. This score is a numeric value.
  • Rows: The dataset contains 1000 rows, each representing an individual student's record with the above attributes.

This detailed breakdown provides a clear understanding of the data columns, which will be essential for analyzing how various factors influence students' test performance.
